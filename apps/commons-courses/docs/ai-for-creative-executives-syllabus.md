## Overview

Creative executives face a widening gap. Consumer-grade AI tools are everywhere, yet most courses either stop at prompting tricks or plunge into engineering complexity that requires an IT partner.

This course closes that gap. It is built specifically for **senior creative leaders** — CCOs, CMOs, VPs of Creative, brand directors, and agency principals — who need to make confident decisions about AI adoption, lead their teams through real workflow change, and understand enough about agentic systems to govern them responsibly.

You will move through the full stack of creative AI:

| Layer | What You'll Cover |
|---|---|
| **Creation** | Multimodal assistants, image, video, and voice generators |
| **Orchestration** | Custom GPTs, no-code automation, agentic pipeline design |
| **Governance** | Provenance, rights, review gates, responsible AI frameworks |

Every lesson is grounded in real tools, real trade-offs, and exercises you can run with your team the following Monday.

## Course Outcomes

- **Lead AI adoption with confidence.** Evaluate tools against a practical executive framework — not hype — and sequence adoption in a way your organisation can absorb.

- **Compress time-to-concept.** Use multimodal assistants to turn a brief into strategic options in hours, not days, with brand voice consistency built in from the start.

- **Prototype across media.** Generate concept boards, animatics, and voice casts in a single sprint — giving your team more directions to explore before committing to production.

- **Understand agentic systems.** Know what AI agents actually are, how a brief-to-publish pipeline works, and how to design human review gates that keep creative quality and legal compliance in the loop.

- **Build repeatable workflows.** Turn one-off AI experiments into organisational capability with no-code automation and structured handoff patterns your team can own.

- **Govern AI output responsibly.** Apply practical frameworks for content provenance, IP and rights review, ethical use, and disclosure — so AI-generated creative can actually ship.

## Learning Experience

The course is structured around a four-part loop that mirrors how creative leaders actually learn and adopt new practices.

```
        ┌──────────────────────────────┐
        │                              │
    SEE ◄──────────────────────── REFLECT
        │                              │
        ▼                              ▲
       TRY ──────────────────────► SHARE
        │                              │
        └──────────────────────────────┘
```

**See** — Each lesson is a focused 15–35 minute video. Instructors explain the *why*, the *what*, and the *how* of a specific tool or concept, illustrated with real examples from creative and marketing organisations.

**Try** — Each module includes a hands-on assignment applied directly to your real creative context — a live brief, a current campaign, or a process your team runs every week. You leave each module with something usable, not just something learned.

**Share** — Discussion prompts and a peer community connect you with other creative leaders working through the same challenges. Compare outputs, trade tool tips, and get feedback on your assignments.

**Reflect** — Structured reflection activities at the end of each module help you map what you've learned back to your specific organisation, team, and upcoming decisions.

## Lesson Plans

---

### Module 1 — The Creative Executive's AI Landscape

*Build the mental model you need to lead AI adoption in a creative organisation — not as a technologist, but as the creative decision-maker.*

**4 LESSONS**

**1. Why AI Changes Creative Leadership** *(20 min — Free Preview)*
This is not about automating creativity — it is about compressing the distance between insight and output. What shifts when your team has AI in the loop at every stage of the creative process.

**2. The Three-Layer Creative AI Stack** *(18 min — Free Preview)*
Creation tools, agentic orchestration, and governance/provenance. How the three layers interact with each other and why understanding all three matters for executives making adoption decisions.

**3. Evaluating AI Tools as a Creative Leader** *(22 min)*
The five criteria that matter for executive adoption: impact leverage, learning cost, workflow reality, governance controls, and provenance readiness. A practical evaluation rubric you can apply to any tool in your stack.

**4. AI Readiness — Where Does Your Organisation Stand?** *(15 min)*
A structured self-assessment. Mapping your team's current AI literacy, tooling gaps, and the highest-leverage entry points for your specific context — team size, workflow maturity, governance environment.

**MODULE 1 ASSIGNMENT**
*AI Readiness Map* — Using the rubric introduced in this module, assess your organisation's current AI readiness across the three layers. Identify your top three entry points and the one blocker most likely to slow adoption.

---

### Module 2 — AI as Your Creative Director of Staff

*Learn to use multimodal AI assistants — ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini — as a creative chief of staff: ideating, synthesising, critiquing, and turning briefs into strategic options.*

**4 LESSONS**

**1. From Brief to Options — The Core Creative Loop** *(25 min)*
A repeatable prompting pattern: brief → strategic framing → concept options → decision checklist. Applied to a real campaign brief from first principles using ChatGPT and Claude side by side.

**2. Prompting for Creative Depth, Not Just Speed** *(22 min)*
Why most executives get mediocre AI outputs: context starvation. System instructions, persona priming, and the five prompting moves that produce genuinely useful creative thinking rather than generic suggestions.

**3. Building Brand Voice Consistency at Scale** *(28 min)*
Using Claude Projects and custom GPTs to encode brand voice, tone guidelines, and approved vocabulary so every output starts from your standards. A walkthrough of building a brand voice system prompt from an existing style guide.

**4. AI-Assisted Research and Competitive Synthesis** *(20 min)*
Turning competitive scans, customer interview transcripts, and trend reports into actionable creative briefs. Perplexity Enterprise, ChatGPT deep research mode, and the critical-evaluation habits you need to keep AI synthesis honest.

**MODULE 2 ASSIGNMENT**
*Brief to Options Sprint* — Take a live brief from your current work and run it through the full brief → options → decision loop using one multimodal assistant. Submit your prompt sequence, three concept options generated, and your evaluation of which worked and why.

---

### Module 3 — Visual, Video, and Voice Exploration

*Move from words to sensory artefacts. Use specialist generators to compress the distance from concept to prototype — mood boards, animatics, and voice casts in hours, not weeks.*

**4 LESSONS**

**1. Image Generation for Brand-Safe Concept Boards** *(30 min)*
Adobe Firefly vs Midjourney — when to use each and why. Generating 20 concept directions inside brand constraints. An introduction to Content Credentials and what they mean for the provenance of your generated assets.

**2. Building a Style Bible with AI** *(25 min)*
A sprint exercise: generate mood boards across three visual directions, evaluate them against creative criteria, select the strongest direction, and translate it into human art direction notes your design team can execute without seeing the AI source.

**3. Video Prototyping for Pitches and Animatics** *(28 min)*
Runway and Pika for short-form motion concepts. The workflow from storyboard to generated clips to rough-cut animatic. What to review before a clip leaves the creative team — consistency, physics, brand safety, and rights.

**4. Voice Casting and Audio Prototyping** *(22 min)*
ElevenLabs for narration, character voices, and localisation exploration. Building a voice casting prototype in a single session. The consent and disclosure workflow your organisation must have in place before any voice clone or synthetic voice is used in a deliverable.

**MODULE 3 ASSIGNMENT**
*Three-Direction Concept Board* — For a current or recent brief, generate a concept board in three distinct visual directions using image generation tools. Select one direction and write a two-paragraph art direction brief based on it — as if briefing a human creative team.

---

### Module 4 — From Prompts to Workflows: Agentic Thinking for Executives

*Understand what AI agents actually are, see how agentic pipelines work in creative production, and build your first no-code automation — without writing a line of code.*

**4 LESSONS**

**1. What Is an AI Agent? (The Non-Technical Explanation)** *(20 min)*
Agents are not chatbots. They plan, call tools, get results, and loop — until a goal is reached. A visual walkthrough of the perceive-reason-act loop, why it is different from prompt-response, and what it changes about creative operations.

**2. The Brief-to-Publish Agentic Pipeline** *(25 min)*
A walkthrough of a real agentic creative pipeline: brief intake → LLM ideation → generator routing → asset assembly → human review gate → provenance attachment → publish and archive. Where the highest-value automation opportunities are and where human judgement remains irreplaceable.

**3. Building Your First Custom GPT for Creative Ops** *(35 min)*
Plan, build, and test a custom GPT that automates a high-frequency creative task — brief intake, copy critique, or brand QA. Hands-on walkthrough from use-case definition through instructions writing, knowledge upload, and first test.

**4. Visual Workflow Automation with n8n** *(30 min)*
Turning a one-off AI experiment into a repeatable team workflow using n8n's visual builder. Brief forms → task creation → AI step → approval routing → archive. Self-hosting vs cloud, designing for failure states, and why n8n gives creative organisations more control than SaaS automation tools.

**MODULE 4 ASSIGNMENT**
*Build One Workflow* — Build either a custom GPT or an n8n workflow that handles a recurring task in your creative operations. Document the use case, the inputs and outputs, and one failure mode you designed for.

---

### Module 5 — Governance, Rights, and Responsible AI

*The reason most AI pilots stall is not capability — it is trust. Build the review gates, provenance practices, and ethical frameworks that let AI-generated creative actually ship.*

**4 LESSONS**

**1. Content Provenance and What It Means for Brands** *(22 min)*
Content Credentials (C2PA) — what they are, which tools attach them, and why they matter for your clients, your legal team, and your reputation. How to write a practical provenance policy for campaigns that use generated assets.

**2. IP, Rights, and Licensing in AI-Generated Creative** *(28 min)*
The current state of copyright in AI-generated work. Training data postures compared: Firefly (licensed content + public domain) vs Midjourney vs Stable Diffusion. Building a rights-review checklist your legal team will actually sign off on — without blocking creative exploration.

**3. Designing Human Review Gates That Scale** *(20 min)*
Review gates are not bottlenecks — they are trust infrastructure. Where to insert human judgement in an agentic pipeline, what to check at each gate, and how to use structured approval tools so review is fast, documented, and auditable.

**4. Ethical Frameworks for AI in Creative Work** *(18 min)*
Bias, representation, and disclosure — the decisions your organisation needs a written policy on before the next campaign ships. A framework for writing your team's responsible AI principles in language your legal, HR, and client-facing teams can all endorse.

**MODULE 5 ASSIGNMENT**
*Rights and Provenance Policy* — Draft a one-page policy for AI-generated assets in your organisation. It should cover: which tools are approved and why, provenance requirements for assets that will be used externally, the rights review checklist, and disclosure standards.

---

### Module 6 — Scaling Creative Operations with AI

*Pilot wins become organisational capability only when you build systems, not habits. Learn how to roll out AI across your creative function and measure what actually matters.*

**4 LESSONS**

**1. Designing Your Creative AI Stack** *(25 min)*
A tool-selection framework for creative organisations: primary multimodal assistant, specialist generators, automation layer, and governance tooling. How to evaluate tools against your specific workflow, sequence adoption over time, and build a budget case for leadership.

**2. Change Management for Creative Teams** *(22 min)*
Creative professionals have legitimate concerns about AI. How to address them honestly, involve your team in tool selection, and move from fear and resistance to agency and experimentation. Real patterns from creative organisation rollouts — what accelerates adoption and what kills it.

**3. Measuring the Impact of AI on Creative Output** *(18 min)*
Cycle time from brief to approved concept. Concepts explored per dollar. Revision rounds before sign-off. Quality scores from clients and stakeholders. The metrics that actually capture AI's impact on creative throughput and output quality — not just cost reduction.

**4. Building Your 90-Day Creative AI Roadmap** *(30 min)*
Capstone lesson: a structured framework for building your organisation's 90-day plan. Define your highest-leverage AI entry point, select your first three tools, design one agentic workflow, write a one-page responsible AI policy for your team, and set the three metrics you will track.

**MODULE 6 ASSIGNMENT — FINAL PROJECT**
*90-Day Creative AI Roadmap* — Compile your work from across all six modules into a single, presentable roadmap. It should include: your AI readiness assessment, the tool stack you are recommending, one agentic workflow design, your rights and provenance policy, and the metrics you will use to measure success. Format it as something you could present to your leadership team or board.
